If you've ever wondered whether a lab-grown diamond is "real," here's the short answer: yes. Chemically, physically, and optically, a lab-grown diamond is the same material as a mined one. Both are crystallized carbon with the same hardness (10 on the Mohs scale) and the same fire.

## So where do they differ?

**Origin.** Mined diamonds form over billions of years underground. Lab-grown diamonds are created in a controlled facility over weeks, using the same pressure-and-heat process nature uses — just accelerated.

**Traceability.** This is the big one for many buyers. A lab-grown stone's full journey — from reactor to finished piece — can be accounted for. That clean, known origin is why so many wearers choose lab-grown as a symbol of a "fresh start."

**Price.** Lab-grown typically costs a fraction of a comparable mined stone, which is why the same budget stretches to a larger or higher-grade stone.

## Which should you choose?

It comes down to what matters to you. If a known, conflict-free origin and better value appeal to you, lab-grown is a strong fit. If you specifically want a geological artifact with billions of years of history, mined may be your choice.
