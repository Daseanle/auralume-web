A piece of jewelry can be more than an accessory — it can be a small, physical reminder of what matters to you. You don't need any belief system for this to work; it's really about giving your attention a place to land.

## A 3-step intention ritual

1. **Choose your word.** Pick one intention — love, abundance, protection, or anything personal. Keep it to a single word or short phrase so it's easy to recall.
2. **Hold the piece.** When you first put it on, take a slow breath and say the word to yourself once. That's it. You're not "charging" the stone; you're anchoring your own attention.
3. **Return to it.** Through the day, when you notice the piece, let it prompt a half-second of focus on your word. Over time, the jewelry becomes a gentle cue.

## Why people find this grounding

A clear, enduring stone is a fitting symbol for something you want to keep close. Wearing it near the heart or throat can be a quiet prompt to stay aligned with your own intention — not a promise that life will change on its own, just a way to keep your focus where you want it.
